Typhoon Disaster Response and Relief Coordination System

Technological Institute of the Philippines - Manila Campus

Introduction Problem Objectives Framework

Introduction

The Philippines frequently faces typhoons that severely impact communities. During such disasters, poor coordination between residents, local governments, and NGOs often leads to duplicated relief efforts and delays in resource distribution. Our system provides a coordinated technology platform to improve speed and efficiency in disaster response.

Problem Statement

In heavily affected areas, inefficient coordination between residents, relief groups, and government units results in slow relief distribution and duplicated efforts. Communication channels are poor, and data is not centralized. A unified platform is needed to streamline coordination and improve disaster response.

Objectives

  • Track affected areas and analyze data for prioritized response.
  • Monitor evacuation centers and optimize shelter allocation.
  • Oversee available resources such as supplies, volunteers, and personnel in real time.

Conceptual Framework

The system connects communities, local governments, and NGOs in one platform. Residents can report damages and urgent needs, LGUs can update affected areas, and NGOs can manage available resources. These inputs are processed in a centralized database, displayed through a real-time disaster map for faster coordination and better decision-making.
